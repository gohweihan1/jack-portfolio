## Featured Projects (4 main)

### Project 01 — BOOK+WORM
- **Category tag:** RE+USE
- **Tagline:** "What if a facade could read the climate like a book?"
- **Location:** NUS E1 Campus Building
- **Collaborator:** Co-designed with Charles Tew Jun Xiang
- **Description:** An adaptive modular winged facade intervention on an existing campus building. The facade performs quiet symbiosis — modulating light, encouraging airflow, and framing communal spaces for reading, learning, and gathering. It reimagines the facade not as a fixed skin, but as a living interface: curious, responsive, and ever-changing.
- **Images:** Pages 4–8 of portfolio (renders, sections, physical model photos)

### Project 02 — SOLITARY+ COMMUNITIES
- **Category tag:** RE+IMAGINE
- **Tagline:** "What if collective living reflected the paradoxes of community + solidarity?"
- **Location:** Tiong Bahru, Singapore (conservation HDBs)
- **Description:** A modular aggregation inspired by the duality of Tiong Bahru's exposed central streets and hidden communal back alleys. Operative Art was used as a generative tool to translate this social paradox into an architecture of solitary peaks and hidden communal spaces.
- **Images:** Pages 9–13

### Project 03 — ARM+(N)ATURE
- **Category tag:** RE+IMAGINE
- **Tagline:** "What if architecture and human anatomy mirror each other in conflict?"
- **Location:** Kallang, Singapore
- **Description:** A study of the human body's extremities as architectural generator. An arm-extension prosthetic was used to gather ergonomic data, translating joint dynamics into structural elements expressing separation, surface, and intensity — housing a contact sports programme.
- **Images:** Pages 14–18

### Project 04 — CERAMICS+COVE
- **Category tag:** RE+VIVE
- **Tagline:** "What if collective living reflected the paradoxes of community + solidarity?"
- **Location:** Kampong Glam, Singapore (on a carpark site)
- **Description:** A mixed-use residential building and pottery studio drawing from Van Eyck's principles and the site's ceramics heritage. The architectural form is generated by the rituals of clay-making — how the process of throwing, coiling, and forming informs spatial movement and order.
- **Images:** Pages 19–23
